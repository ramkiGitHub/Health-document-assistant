# Appointment Scheduling And Cancellation Policy

## Document Status

- Document type: Synthetic healthcare policy
- Intended use: Training and demo use only
- Contains real patient data: No
- Effective date: January 1, 2026
- Department: Patient Access Services

## Purpose

This policy defines how patients may schedule, reschedule, cancel, and confirm outpatient appointments. The goal is to support timely access to care, reduce missed appointments, and ensure appointment slots are used efficiently.

## Scope

This policy applies to outpatient clinic visits, follow-up consultations, diagnostic service appointments, and telehealth appointments. It does not apply to emergency department visits or inpatient admissions.

## Scheduling A New Appointment

Patients may schedule a new appointment through the patient portal, call center, clinic front desk, or approved referral channels. The scheduling team must collect the patient's full name, date of birth, contact number, appointment reason, preferred clinic location, and insurance information when applicable.

For referral-based appointments, the scheduling team must confirm that a valid referral or authorization is available before confirming the visit. If authorization is missing, the appointment may be marked as pending until the required information is received.

## Appointment Confirmation

Patients should receive an appointment confirmation by SMS, email, portal notification, or phone call. The confirmation should include appointment date, time, clinic location, provider name when available, preparation instructions, and cancellation instructions.

Patients are encouraged to confirm their appointment at least 24 hours before the scheduled time. If the patient does not confirm, the appointment remains active unless cancelled by the patient or clinic.

## Rescheduling Policy

Patients may reschedule an appointment up to 24 hours before the scheduled time without penalty. Same-day rescheduling is allowed when clinic availability permits, but repeated same-day changes may require review by the clinic coordinator.

If the appointment requires special preparation, such as fasting or medication instructions, the scheduling team must provide updated preparation guidance after rescheduling.

## Cancellation Policy

Patients should cancel appointments at least 24 hours before the scheduled time. Cancellations may be made through the patient portal, call center, or clinic front desk.

If a patient cancels less than 24 hours before the appointment, the cancellation is considered late. Late cancellation fees may apply only when allowed by clinic policy and payer rules.

## No-Show Policy

A no-show occurs when a patient misses an appointment without cancelling before the scheduled start time. After two no-shows within six months, the clinic may require phone confirmation before scheduling future appointments.

Patients should not be denied medically necessary care solely because of prior no-shows. The clinic coordinator must review access concerns before any scheduling restrictions are applied.

## Telehealth Appointments

For telehealth appointments, patients should join the virtual visit at least 10 minutes before the scheduled time. The patient must be in a private location and have a stable internet connection when possible.

If the patient cannot connect within 15 minutes after the appointment start time, the visit may be rescheduled. Technical issues should be documented in the appointment notes.

## Late Arrival Policy

Patients who arrive more than 15 minutes late may be asked to wait, see another available provider, or reschedule. The clinic should consider appointment type, provider availability, and patient needs before rescheduling a late patient.

## Required Documentation

For the first visit, patients should bring a photo ID, insurance card, referral or authorization when required, medication list, and relevant medical records. For follow-up visits, patients should bring updated insurance information and any new test results.

## Escalation

Scheduling conflicts, urgent access needs, repeated no-shows, or unresolved authorization issues should be escalated to the clinic coordinator. Emergency symptoms should not be scheduled as routine appointments and should be directed to emergency services.

## Safety Notice

This synthetic policy is for learning and demonstration only. It is not medical advice and should not be used for real clinical operations.
