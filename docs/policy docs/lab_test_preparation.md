# Laboratory Test Preparation Instructions

## Document Status

- Document type: Synthetic healthcare instructions
- Intended use: Training and demo use only
- Contains real patient data: No
- Effective date: January 1, 2026
- Department: Laboratory Services

## Purpose

This document provides general preparation instructions for common laboratory tests. The goal is to help patients arrive prepared and reduce delays, cancelled tests, or inaccurate samples.

## Scope

This document applies to routine blood tests, fasting blood tests, urine tests, stool tests, and specimen collection appointments. It does not replace instructions from a licensed clinician.

## General Preparation

Patients should bring a photo ID, insurance card, lab order, and list of current medications when requested. Patients should arrive at least 10 minutes before the appointment time.

Patients should follow the specific instructions provided by their ordering clinician or laboratory. If instructions conflict, the patient should contact the ordering clinician or laboratory before the test.

## Fasting Blood Tests

Some blood tests may require fasting. If fasting is required, patients should not eat or drink anything except water for the instructed fasting period. Common fasting periods are 8 to 12 hours, depending on the test and clinician instructions.

Patients should not stop prescribed medications unless instructed by a licensed clinician. Questions about medication before a fasting test should be escalated to the ordering clinician.

## Hydration

Patients may drink water before most blood tests unless instructed otherwise. Staying hydrated may make blood collection easier.

Patients with fluid restrictions should follow their clinician's instructions.

## Diabetes Considerations

Patients with diabetes should ask their clinician for specific instructions before fasting. The laboratory staff should not advise patients to change insulin, oral diabetes medication, or meal plans.

Questions about fasting safety for diabetes should be escalated to the ordering clinician.

## Urine Tests

For urine tests, patients may be asked to provide a clean-catch sample. Staff should provide collection instructions and a sterile container.

Some urine tests require first-morning samples or timed collection. Patients should follow the specific collection instructions provided.

## Stool Tests

For stool tests, patients should use the collection kit provided by the laboratory. The sample should not be mixed with urine, toilet water, or cleaning products.

The sample should be returned within the timeframe stated in the collection instructions.

## Medication And Supplements

Some tests may be affected by medications or supplements. Patients should not stop medications unless instructed by a licensed clinician. Patients should bring a medication and supplement list when requested.

## Late Arrival Or Missed Appointment

Patients who arrive late may need to wait or reschedule depending on sample timing and laboratory capacity. Tests requiring timed collection may need to be rescheduled if the timing window is missed.

## Escalation

Questions involving medication changes, fasting safety, pregnancy, diabetes, fainting risk, fluid restriction, or unclear clinician instructions should be escalated to the ordering clinician or laboratory supervisor.

## Safety Notice

This synthetic document is for learning and demonstration only. It is not medical advice and should not replace clinician instructions.
