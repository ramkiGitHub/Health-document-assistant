# Insurance Claims Submission Policy

## Document Status

- Document type: Synthetic healthcare policy
- Intended use: Training and demo use only
- Contains real patient data: No
- Effective date: January 1, 2026
- Department: Revenue Cycle Management

## Purpose

This policy describes the documentation and workflow required to submit healthcare insurance claims. The goal is to improve claim accuracy, reduce denials, and support timely reimbursement.

## Scope

This policy applies to outpatient visits, inpatient services, laboratory services, diagnostic imaging, and approved telehealth encounters. It applies to claims submitted to commercial insurance plans, government payers, and employer-sponsored health plans.

## Required Claim Information

Each insurance claim must include the patient's full name, date of birth, insurance policy number, payer name, date of service, provider name, facility name, diagnosis code, procedure code, billed amount, and place of service.

Claims may also require prior authorization number, referral number, discharge summary, clinical notes, provider invoice, lab report, imaging report, or proof of medical necessity depending on payer requirements.

## Patient Responsibility

Patients are responsible for providing accurate insurance information before or during registration. If coverage changes, the patient should notify the registration team before services are provided.

Patients may be asked to provide a copy of the insurance card, photo ID, referral form, authorization letter, coordination of benefits information, or secondary insurance details.

## Provider Documentation

Providers must complete clinical documentation within the required documentation window. The documentation should support the diagnosis, service performed, medical necessity, and level of care.

Incomplete provider documentation may delay claim submission. If documentation is missing, the billing team should send a clarification request to the provider or coding team.

## Claim Submission Timeline

Clean claims should be submitted within five business days after coding is complete. Claims requiring additional review should be resolved within ten business days when possible.

Timely filing limits vary by payer. The billing team must check payer-specific filing deadlines before submission.

## Claim Review

Before submission, the billing team should verify patient demographics, insurance eligibility, authorization requirements, diagnosis codes, procedure codes, provider identifiers, and payer-specific claim rules.

Claims with missing information should be placed in pending status and routed to the responsible team for correction.

## Denial Management

If a claim is denied, the billing team must review the denial reason, payer remark code, documentation, and submission history. Common denial reasons include missing authorization, invalid policy number, coding mismatch, duplicate claim, lack of medical necessity, and timely filing issues.

Appeals should include supporting documentation such as clinical notes, authorization proof, corrected codes, invoices, and payer correspondence.

## Coordination Of Benefits

When a patient has more than one insurance plan, the billing team must identify the primary and secondary payer before claim submission. Incorrect payer order may result in claim denial or payment delay.

## Patient Billing

Patients should not be billed until insurance processing is complete unless the service is known to be non-covered or self-pay. Patient statements should clearly show insurance payment, adjustment, deductible, copay, coinsurance, and remaining balance.

## Escalation

Claims with repeated denials, missing authorization, high billed amounts, payer disputes, or unclear medical necessity should be escalated to the revenue cycle supervisor.

## Safety Notice

This synthetic policy is for learning and demonstration only. It is not legal, billing, or medical advice.
