# Patient Privacy And Information Sharing Policy

## Document Status

- Document type: Synthetic healthcare policy
- Intended use: Training and demo use only
- Contains real patient data: No
- Effective date: January 1, 2026
- Department: Compliance And Privacy Office

## Purpose

This policy describes how patient information should be protected, accessed, disclosed, and handled in a healthcare setting. The goal is to support privacy, confidentiality, and responsible information use.

## Scope

This policy applies to workforce members, contractors, volunteers, students, and approved third parties who access patient information through clinical systems, billing systems, paper records, messages, or verbal communication.

## Protected Information

Protected information may include patient name, date of birth, address, phone number, medical record number, insurance details, diagnosis, treatment information, medication history, appointment details, billing information, and test results.

Protected information should be accessed only when required for treatment, payment, healthcare operations, or another approved purpose.

## Minimum Necessary Standard

Workforce members should access, use, and disclose only the minimum information needed to complete an approved task. Accessing patient records out of curiosity or for personal reasons is prohibited.

When responding to routine requests, staff should share only the specific information required rather than the entire record.

## Patient Consent And Authorization

Patient authorization may be required before disclosing records to employers, schools, legal representatives, family members, or outside organizations. Authorization should identify what information may be shared, who may receive it, the purpose of disclosure, and the expiration date.

Some disclosures may be allowed without written authorization when permitted by law or policy, such as treatment coordination, payment processing, public health reporting, or emergency circumstances.

## Identity Verification

Before discussing patient information by phone, portal message, or in person, staff must verify identity using approved identifiers. Acceptable identifiers may include full name, date of birth, address, phone number, or medical record number.

Staff should not disclose sensitive information until identity is reasonably confirmed.

## Family And Caregiver Communication

Patients may identify family members or caregivers who are allowed to receive certain information. Staff should confirm the patient's preference and document communication permissions when required.

If communication permissions are unclear, staff should escalate to the privacy office or supervisor before sharing information.

## Secure Communication

Patient information should be sent only through approved secure channels, such as the patient portal, encrypted email, approved fax process, or secure internal messaging system.

Patient information should not be sent through personal email accounts, personal messaging apps, public chat tools, or unsecured file-sharing services.

## Breach Reporting

Suspected privacy incidents must be reported promptly to the privacy office. Examples include misdirected emails, lost documents, unauthorized record access, incorrect patient messages, or accidental disclosure.

The privacy office will review the incident, determine required actions, and coordinate notifications if necessary.

## AI And Patient Data

Real patient information must not be entered into unapproved AI tools. AI systems used for healthcare operations must follow organizational privacy, security, retention, and vendor review requirements.

Synthetic or de-identified data should be used for training, demos, and testing whenever possible.

## Escalation

Questions about record disclosure, patient authorization, legal requests, suspected breaches, or AI use with patient information should be escalated to the privacy office.

## Safety Notice

This synthetic policy is for learning and demonstration only. It is not legal advice and should not be used as a real privacy policy.
