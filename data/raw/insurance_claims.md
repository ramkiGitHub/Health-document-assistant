# Insurance Claims Processing

## Overview
This document outlines the procedures for filing insurance claims and the requirements for claim submission.

## Claim Submission Requirements

### Required Documents
All claims must include the following:
1. **Claim Form**: Completed CMS-1500 (for non-institutional) or UB-04 (for facility)
2. **Policy Information**: Insurance policy number and member ID
3. **Provider Information**: NPI (National Provider Identifier) and tax ID
4. **Service Documentation**: 
   - Date of service
   - Diagnosis codes (ICD-10)
   - Procedure codes (CPT)
5. **Invoice or Receipt**: Itemized statement of charges
6. **Provider Credentials**: Copy of provider license and credentials verification

### Additional Documents by Claim Type

#### Inpatient Claims
- Hospital discharge summary
- Itemized hospital bill
- Procedure notes
- Lab and imaging results
- Medication list during stay

#### Outpatient Claims
- Office visit notes
- Test results if applicable
- Prescription documentation

#### Specialist Referral Claims
- Referral authorization number
- Referring provider information
- Specialist credentials

## Claim Submission Process

### Step 1: Gather Documentation
- Collect all required documents listed above
- Ensure all forms are completed accurately
- Verify insurance eligibility

### Step 2: Submit Claim
Submit via one of these methods:
- **Electronic**: EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) submission
- **Online**: Insurance portal submission
- **Mail**: Send to claims department address
- **Fax**: To designated fax number (verify number with insurer)

### Step 3: Claim Tracking
- Obtain claim reference/confirmation number
- Track claim status online or via customer service
- Expected processing time: 30-45 days

## Claim Processing Timeline

| Timeline | Event |
|----------|-------|
| 0 days | Claim submitted |
| 1-5 days | Initial verification and data entry |
| 6-10 days | Clinical review (if needed) |
| 11-30 days | Determination and adjudication |
| 31-45 days | EOB (Explanation of Benefits) mailed |

## Common Claim Issues

### Claim Denials
Common reasons for denial:
- Missing required documentation
- Non-covered service
- Out-of-network provider (verify before service)
- Pre-authorization not obtained
- Duplicate claim submission

### How to Appeal
1. Obtain EOB with denial reason
2. Gather supporting documentation
3. Submit appeal within 90 days of denial
4. Include appeal letter and documentation
5. Allow 30 days for appeal review

## Insurance Eligibility

### Verifying Coverage
- Check policy for covered services
- Verify if pre-authorization is required
- Confirm in-network vs out-of-network status
- Review deductible and copay amounts

### Pre-Authorization Requirements
Services requiring pre-authorization:
- Major surgical procedures
- Specialty consultations
- Diagnostic imaging (MRI, CT scans)
- Physical therapy (usually > 10 visits)
- Mental health services
- Durable medical equipment

### Pre-Authorization Process
1. Provider requests authorization from insurance
2. Insurance reviews medical necessity
3. Authorization number issued if approved
4. Patient receives confirmation

## Appeals and Disputes

### Appeal Timeline
- Initial appeal: Within 90 days of EOB
- Secondary appeal: Within 180 days of denial
- Final determination: Within 60 days of final appeal

### Required for Appeal
- Claim reference number
- Clear reason for appeal
- New medical evidence or documentation
- Detailed explanation of dispute
