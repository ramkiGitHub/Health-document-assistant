# Hospital Discharge Guidelines

## Overview
These guidelines establish procedures for safe and effective patient discharge from the hospital, ensuring continuity of care and optimal patient outcomes.

## Pre-Discharge Planning

### Discharge Planning Process
- Begins upon admission
- Involves physician, nurses, social workers, and patient
- Addresses medical, social, and support needs
- Identifies barriers to recovery at home
- Develops discharge plan before patient leaves hospital

### Key Questions to Address
1. What is patient's home environment?
2. Can patient manage self-care?
3. Does patient live alone?
4. Are family/caregivers available?
5. What support services might be needed?
6. What is patient's financial situation?
7. Are transportation arrangements needed?
8. Does patient have prescriptions and medications?

## Discharge Criteria

### When Patient is Ready for Discharge
- Vital signs stable and at baseline
- Pain controlled adequately
- Able to eat and drink normally
- Bowel and bladder function normal
- Infection resolved or managed
- Wound healing appropriately (if applicable)
- Able to perform activities of daily living
- Understands discharge instructions
- Follow-up care arranged

### Physician Discharge Order
- Physician must write discharge order
- Order includes:
  - Discharge date and time
  - Medications to continue
  - Restrictions and activity level
  - Follow-up appointments
  - Dietary restrictions
  - Wound care instructions
  - When to seek emergency care

## Discharge Instructions

### Essential Information Provided
All discharge instructions include:

**Medications**
- Exact name, dose, and frequency
- When to start taking
- How to take (with food, timing)
- Potential side effects
- When to contact provider about side effects

**Activity and Rest**
- When safe to return to work
- Activity restrictions or limitations
- When to resume exercise or sports
- Sexual activity guidelines if applicable
- When to drive if relevant to surgery/condition

**Diet**
- Any dietary restrictions to follow
- Foods to avoid
- Foods to increase
- Hydration requirements
- When normal diet can resume

**Wound Care** (if applicable)
- How to keep wound clean and dry
- When to change dressings
- Signs of infection (fever, redness, drainage)
- When sutures/staples are removed
- Activity restrictions related to wound

**Pain Management**
- Prescribed pain medications and dosing
- Over-the-counter pain relief options
- Non-medication pain relief methods
- Expected pain trajectory
- When to contact provider about pain

**Warning Signs Requiring Immediate Care**
- Fever > 101.5°F (38.6°C)
- Increased redness, warmth, or drainage from wound
- Inability to keep food or fluids down
- Severe abdominal pain
- Chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Sudden severe headache
- Signs of stroke or TIA
- Other serious symptoms specific to condition

### When to Contact Provider
- Mild fever (100.4-101.4°F)
- Increased pain despite medications
- Swelling that doesn't improve with elevation
- Rash or itching related to medications
- Nausea or constipation
- Questions about medications or instructions
- Concerns about recovery

### When to Seek Emergency Care
- Call 911 or go to nearest emergency department if:
  - High fever with confusion
  - Severe chest pain
  - Difficulty breathing
  - Uncontrolled bleeding
  - Signs of stroke
  - Severe allergic reaction
  - Traumatic injury

## Follow-Up Care

### Appointment Scheduling
- Discharge coordinator schedules follow-up appointments
- Primary care appointment within 1-2 weeks
- Specialist appointments as needed
- Patient receives appointment confirmations
- Transportation arranged if needed

### What to Bring to Follow-Up Visits
- Insurance card and photo ID
- List of current medications
- Discharge paperwork and instructions
- Symptom log if requested
- Questions or concerns written down

### Continuity of Care
- Discharge summary sent to primary care provider
- Medication list provided to all providers
- Test results and imaging available to providers
- Communication between hospital and providers

## Post-Discharge Support

### Home Care Services (if ordered)
- Visiting nurse for wound care or medication management
- Physical therapy or rehabilitation
- Speech therapy if applicable
- Occupational therapy for activities of daily living
- Home health aide for personal care

### Support Services Available
- Dietary counseling
- Mental health counseling
- Financial assistance programs
- Community resources
- Caregiver support programs
- Disease-specific support groups

### What to Expect at Home
- Fatigue and need for increased rest
- Emotional adjustments to hospitalization
- Normal anxiety or sadness during recovery
- Gradual return to normal activities
- Healing takes time (varies by condition)

## Medication Management Post-Discharge

### Starting New Medications
- Start with exact dose prescribed
- Take at scheduled times
- Do not skip doses
- Keep appointment with pharmacist for questions

### Stopping Previous Medications
- Follow physician instructions
- Some medications can be stopped immediately
- Others need gradual dose reduction
- Ask about tapering if needed
- Do not stop without consulting provider

### Managing Multiple Medications
- Use pill organizer or reminder system
- Keep updated medication list
- Bring all medications to follow-up visits
- Ask pharmacist about interactions

## Special Discharge Circumstances

### Extended Care Facility Placement
- For patients unable to go home
- Includes skilled nursing, rehabilitation, or long-term care
- Arranged during hospitalization
- Family involvement in facility selection

### Home with Caregiver
- Caregiver education provided
- Training on wound care or medications
- Support resources for caregiver
- Respite care options

### Patients Without Stable Housing
- Social work consultation
- Emergency shelter referrals
- Transition planning
- Follow-up arrangements

## Patient Education and Safety

### Understanding Discharge Instructions
- Patient encouraged to ask questions
- Information provided in understandable language
- Written instructions given to patient
- Printed in patient's preferred language when available

### Medication Education
- Nurse reviews each medication
- Demonstrates any injections or special techniques
- Provides written information about medications
- Patient demonstrates understanding

### Self-Care Skills Training
- Demonstrates wound care if needed
- Reviews exercise or physical therapy
- Discusses dietary modifications
- Provides resources for continued learning

## Checklist Before Discharge

Before patient leaves, ensure:
- [ ] Discharge order signed by physician
- [ ] Patient has written discharge instructions
- [ ] Prescriptions written and ready to fill
- [ ] Medications patient brought to hospital returned or arranged
- [ ] Follow-up appointments scheduled
- [ ] Transportation arranged
- [ ] Home care services arranged if needed
- [ ] Patient/family education completed
- [ ] Patient demonstrates understanding of instructions
- [ ] Discharge summary ready for sending to providers
- [ ] Patient has contact information for questions
- [ ] Patient knows when to seek emergency care

## Post-Discharge Contact

### 24-Hour Follow-Up
- Nurse may call to check on patient
- Assessment of how patient is doing at home
- Reinforcement of instructions
- Identification of any concerns

### One-Week Follow-Up
- Ensure follow-up appointment scheduled
- Assess medication side effects
- Answer questions about recovery
- Identify any complications
