# Medication Safety Guidelines

## Overview
These guidelines ensure safe medication use, reduce medication errors, and promote patient safety and health outcomes.

## Medication Storage

### Temperature Control
- Store most medications at room temperature (68-77°F / 20-25°C)
- Check medication labels for specific storage requirements
- Refrigerated medications should be stored at 36-46°F (2-8°C)
- Do not freeze medications unless instructed
- Keep medications away from heat, moisture, and direct sunlight

### Proper Storage Locations
- Keep in original prescription bottles
- Store in a cool, dry place
- Keep in a locked cabinet if children or pets are present
- Do not store in bathroom medicine cabinets (humidity)
- Do not store in kitchen cabinets near stoves (heat)

## Medication Adherence

### Taking Medications Correctly
1. **Read Labels**: Check medication name, dose, frequency, and expiration date
2. **Follow Instructions**: Take exactly as prescribed
3. **Set Reminders**: Use phone alarms, pill organizers, or calendar
4. **Record Taking**: Keep a medication log
5. **Never Skip Doses**: Unless directed by healthcare provider

### Timing of Medications
- Some medications should be taken with food; others on empty stomach
- Some medications interact if taken close together (check 2+ hour spacing)
- Consistency is important for medications taken daily
- Take at the same time each day for best results

## Drug Interactions and Warnings

### Before Taking New Medication, Inform Provider About:
- All other medications you are taking (including over-the-counter)
- All supplements and herbal products
- Alcohol or tobacco use
- Allergies or adverse reactions to medications
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding status
- Liver or kidney disease

### Common Drug Interactions
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) with blood thinners: Increased bleeding risk
- Statins with certain antibiotics: Increased muscle damage risk
- ACE inhibitors with potassium supplements: Dangerous potassium levels
- Antacids with certain medications: Reduced effectiveness

### Alcohol Interactions
Do not mix with:
- Sedatives and sleep aids (increased drowsiness)
- Pain medications (increased risk of overdose)
- Diabetes medications (dangerous blood sugar levels)
- Blood pressure medications (severe drops in blood pressure)

## Medication Side Effects

### Common Side Effects (Usually Mild)
- Nausea or upset stomach
- Headache
- Dizziness
- Mild fatigue
- Dry mouth

### Serious Side Effects (Contact Provider Immediately)
- Severe allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing)
- Chest pain
- Irregular heartbeat
- Severe headache or dizziness
- Unexplained bleeding or bruising
- Severe abdominal pain

## Prescription Management

### When Picking Up Prescriptions
1. Verify patient name and date of birth
2. Check medication name and strength
3. Confirm number of pills and refills allowed
4. Ask pharmacist about side effects and interactions
5. Keep prescription records

### Prescription Refills
- Request refills 2-3 days before running out
- Ask if medication can be refilled or if new prescription needed
- Some medications have refill limits per year
- Some medications cannot be refilled (must see doctor for new prescription)

### Expired Medications
- Check expiration dates before taking
- Do not use expired medications (may be ineffective or unsafe)
- Properly dispose of expired medications (ask pharmacist)
- Do not flush medications down toilet or throw in trash

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy and Breastfeeding
- Inform provider if pregnant or breastfeeding
- Some medications harm fetus or pass into breast milk
- Benefits vs. risks must be evaluated
- Never stop medication without consulting provider

### Elderly Patients
- Review medications with provider yearly
- Simplify medication schedule if possible
- Risk of interactions increases with multiple medications
- Fall risk may increase with certain medications

### Children
- Always measure liquid medication with a measuring cup or syringe
- Never use kitchen spoons for measuring
- Keep medications out of reach of children
- Use child-proof bottles

## Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medication Safety

### Common OTC Medications
- Pain relievers: Acetaminophen (Tylenol), Ibuprofen (Advil), Naproxen (Aleve)
- Cold/flu: Decongestants, antihistamines, cough suppressants
- Antacids: Calcium carbonate, magnesium hydroxide
- Laxatives: Stool softeners, bulk-formers

### OTC Drug Interactions
- OTC pain relievers can interact with prescription medications
- Multiple products may contain same active ingredient (risk of overdose)
- Always read labels and ask pharmacist before combining OTC products

## Medication Administration

### Routes of Administration
- **Oral**: By mouth (most common)
- **Injection**: Intravenous (IV), intramuscular (IM), subcutaneous
- **Topical**: On skin (creams, patches)
- **Inhalation**: Breathed into lungs (inhalers, nebulizers)
- **Rectal**: Suppositories
- **Sublingual**: Under tongue

### Proper Technique
1. Wash hands before administering
2. Verify patient identity
3. Check medication name, dose, expiration
4. Use sterile technique if applicable
5. Document administration
6. Observe for adverse reactions

## Emergency Situations

### Medication Overdose
- Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
- Call 911 if unconscious or severe symptoms
- Have medication bottle available for identification
- Do not induce vomiting unless directed

### Medication Allergies
- Inform all healthcare providers of allergies
- Wear medical alert bracelet if severe allergies
- Ask pharmacist before taking new medications

## Questions to Ask Your Pharmacist

1. What is this medication for?
2. How do I take it (dose, frequency, timing)?
3. What should I do if I miss a dose?
4. What are possible side effects?
5. Can I take it with food or alcohol?
6. Will it interact with my other medications?
7. When should I expect improvement?
8. What foods/activities should I avoid?
9. How should I store this medication?
10. How long should I take this medication?
