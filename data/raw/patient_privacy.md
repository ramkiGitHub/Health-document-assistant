# Patient Privacy and Data Protection Policy

## Overview
This policy establishes guidelines to protect patient privacy and comply with HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) regulations.

## Protected Health Information (PHI)

### What is PHI?
Protected Health Information includes any information in a medical record that can identify a patient:
- Name, address, phone number, email
- Social Security number
- Date of birth
- Medical record number
- Insurance information
- Health conditions and diagnoses
- Medications and allergies
- Test results and lab values
- Photos or recordings

### Patient Rights Regarding PHI
- Right to access their medical records
- Right to request corrections to records
- Right to know who has accessed their records
- Right to request restricted use of information
- Right to receive notice of privacy practices
- Right to file complaints regarding privacy violations

## Access to Medical Records

### How to Request Records
1. Complete authorization form (available at front desk or online)
2. Specify what records you want copies of
3. Indicate where copies should be sent
4. Sign and date the form

### Processing Time
- Routine requests: 10 business days
- Complex requests: 30 business days
- Expedited requests (additional fee may apply): 5 business days

### Fees
- First 30 pages: Free
- Additional pages: $0.50-$1.00 per page
- Electronic records copy: $10-$20
- Certified copies: $2-$5 additional

## Authorized Users of PHI

### Who Can Access Medical Records Without Permission
- Treating physicians and nurses
- Medical staff involved in care
- Billing and insurance staff
- Quality improvement and safety staff
- Legal and compliance personnel
- Public health authorities (reporting requirements)
- Law enforcement (with warrant)

### Who Needs Written Authorization
- Family members (unless designated)
- Employers
- Life insurance companies
- Attorneys
- Researchers
- Anyone else not listed above

## Patient Consent and Authorization

### Treatment Consent
- Patient must consent before treatment
- Consent covers discussion and testing
- Separate consent required for procedures and surgery
- Right to refuse treatment

### Authorization for Release
- Specific authorization required to share information
- Authorization form must specify:
  - Information to be released
  - To whom it will be released
  - Reason for release
  - Date authorization signed
  - Expiration date

### Revocation of Authorization
- Patient can revoke authorization at any time
- Revocation does not affect actions taken before revocation
- Submit revocation in writing

## Security and Confidentiality

### Physical Security Measures
- Medical records stored in locked cabinets
- Staff workstations require password protection
- Computer screens positioned away from public view
- After-hours security systems activated
- Visitor sign-in and ID verification

### Data Security Measures
- Patient data encrypted during transmission
- Secure password requirements for all staff
- Multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems
- Regular security audits and updates
- Firewalls and antivirus protection
- Backup systems and disaster recovery

### Staff Training
- Annual HIPAA and privacy training required
- Confidentiality agreements signed by all staff
- Consequences for privacy violations
- Regular updates on privacy regulations

## Breach Notification

### What Constitutes a Breach
- Unauthorized access, use, or disclosure of PHI
- Lost or stolen records or devices
- Accidental email to wrong recipient
- Unencrypted data transmission
- Unattended records left in public areas

### Breach Response Procedures
1. Immediately contain the breach
2. Assess risk to patient privacy
3. Document details of the breach
4. Investigate and determine scope
5. Notify affected patients within 60 days
6. Notify media if more than 500 residents affected
7. Report to HHS (Health and Human Services)
8. Implement corrective actions

### Breach Notification Content
- What happened and how many people affected
- What information was involved
- When breach was discovered
- Steps being taken to investigate
- Recommendations for patient protection
- Contact information for questions

## HIPAA Compliance

### HIPAA Requirements
- Privacy Rule: Controls use and disclosure of PHI
- Security Rule: Protects electronic health information
- Breach Notification Rule: Requires notification if breach occurs
- HIPAA Enforcement: OCR (Office for Civil Rights) investigates violations

### Penalties for Violations
- Civil penalties: $100-$50,000 per violation
- Criminal penalties: Fines and imprisonment for intentional violations
- Organizational liability for employee violations

## Patient Rights - Detailed

### Right to Access
- Patient has right to see and obtain copies of medical records
- Request must be fulfilled within 30 days

### Right to Amendment
- Patient can request corrections or additions to records
- Provider must respond within 60 days
- If denied, patient can file written statement

### Right to Accounting of Disclosures
- Patient can request list of who has accessed their records
- Last 6 years of disclosures included
- Some disclosures may be excluded

### Right to Request Restrictions
- Patient can request limits on use or disclosure
- Provider can accept or deny request
- If accepted, provider must honor restrictions

### Right to Confidential Communications
- Patient can request records be sent to different address
- Patient can receive care anonymously if paying out of pocket

## Special Situations

### Minors and Guardianship
- Parent/guardian can access minor's records unless:
  - Minor is authorized to consent to treatment
  - Abuse or harm by parent/guardian suspected
  - Court order restricts access

### Deceased Patients
- Records released to executor or authorized representative
- Information cannot be used for purposes other than permitted
- Generally held for 5-7 years after death

### Substance Abuse and Mental Health
- Federal regulations 42 CFR Part 2 provide additional protection
- Separate written consent required for disclosure
- Stricter penalties for unauthorized disclosure
