# Laboratory Test Preparation Guidelines

## Overview
Proper preparation before laboratory tests ensures accurate results and optimal patient experience. These guidelines cover preparation requirements for common laboratory tests.

## General Laboratory Preparation

### Before Your Test
- Ask your healthcare provider what preparation is needed
- Ask if you need to fast (not eat or drink)
- Ask what medications to take or hold
- Ask what time to arrive
- Ask where to go and how long the test takes
- Write down instructions or request written information

### What to Bring
- Photo ID
- Insurance card
- Authorization or referral from your doctor
- List of current medications
- Any previous test results if requested

### Arrival
- Arrive at least 15 minutes early
- Check in at front desk
- Verify patient information
- Ask any remaining questions

## Fasting Blood Tests

### Why Fasting is Required
- Food affects certain blood values
- Fat in food can affect lipid panel results
- Blood glucose affected by recent eating
- Some medication timing affects results

### Fasting Requirements
- Nothing to eat or drink (except water) for 9-12 hours
- Typically fast after dinner the night before morning appointment
- Can drink plain water during fast
- Do not chew gum or mints (contains sugar)
- Do not smoke (nicotine can affect results)

### Common Fasting Blood Tests
- Fasting glucose (blood sugar)
- Lipid panel (cholesterol, triglycerides)
- Liver function tests
- Kidney function tests
- Thyroid function tests
- Comprehensive metabolic panel

### Breaking Your Fast
- Eat and drink normally after blood draw
- Bring snack if you feel dizzy after fast

## Blood Draw Preparation

### Before Blood Draw
- Stay hydrated (drink water) day before
- Avoid strenuous exercise day of test
- Wear loose-fitting clothing for easy arm access
- Avoid caffeine if possible (can elevate heart rate)

### During Blood Draw
- Inform phlebotomist of needle anxiety or difficulty
- Inform of history of fainting
- Sit comfortably in chair
- Avoid looking at needle if anxious
- Take deep breaths to relax

### After Blood Draw
- Apply pressure to puncture site for 2-3 minutes
- Can apply bandage if needed
- Bruising is normal and usually fades in 2-3 days
- Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours

## Urine Test Preparation

### Collection Requirements
- Use sterile container provided
- Mid-stream collection preferred
- For females: wipe front to back first
- For males: clean urethral area if uncircumcised
- Do not touch inside of container

### What to Avoid
- Avoid touching container interior
- Do not contaminate with feces
- Collect before bowel movement or after wiping
- For women: do not collect during menstruation unless instructed

### Timing
- First morning urine often preferred (more concentrated)
- Can collect at other times if specified
- Use collection within 2 hours of collection if not refrigerated

### For 24-Hour Collection
- Use container provided (usually with preservative)
- Collect all urine for 24-hour period
- Discard first morning void
- Refrigerate collection if instructed
- Bring full container to lab

## Specific Test Preparations

### Lipid Panel (Cholesterol)
- Fast 9-12 hours
- Blood draw usually in morning
- Accurate measurement of cholesterol and triglycerides

### Glucose Tolerance Test
- Fast overnight (8-12 hours)
- Fasting blood draw
- Drink sugary solution
- Blood draws at timed intervals (usually 1, 2, 3 hours)
- Duration approximately 3 hours
- Bring snack for after test

### Thyroid Function Tests (TSH, Free T4)
- No special preparation usually needed
- Can eat and drink normally
- Some medications affect results (inform provider)
- Best tested in morning

### Liver Function Tests (LFTs)
- May require fasting (ask provider)
- Blood draw only
- No special activity restrictions

### Kidney Function Tests (Creatinine, BUN)
- May require fasting
- Blood draw and/or urine test
- Important to stay hydrated

### Prostate Cancer Screening (PSA)
- No special preparation
- Can eat and drink normally
- Note: recent ejaculation can raise levels

### Hemoglobin A1C (Diabetes screening)
- No fasting required
- Can eat and drink normally
- Reflects average blood sugar over 2-3 months
- Can be drawn anytime

## Medication Management Before Tests

### Medications to Continue
- Ask provider which medications to take normally
- Most blood pressure and heart medications continue
- Most anxiety medications continue
- Take with sip of water if needed

### Medications to Hold
- Some blood thinners may need temporary hold
- Some diabetes medications may be adjusted
- Some cholesterol medications may affect certain tests
- Always ask provider specifically

### Medication Timing
- If must take medication before test, eat light snack
- Take medication with small amount of water
- Inform lab staff of medications you took before test

## Physical Preparation

### Day Before Test
- Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
- Avoid excessive caffeine
- Stay well-hydrated (drink plenty of water)
- Avoid alcohol 24 hours before (affects liver and lipid tests)
- Avoid strenuous exercise
- Manage stress

### Day of Test
- Avoid excessive caffeine if possible
- Stay calm and relaxed
- Arrive early to reduce stress
- Take deep breaths
- If nervous, inform staff for support

## Stool Sample Collection

### Requirements
- Use clean container provided
- Collect sample of stool
- Do not contaminate with urine or toilet water
- Use toothpick-sized applicator if provided
- Place in container quickly

### Timing
- Refrigerate if not brought to lab immediately
- Deliver to lab within 24 hours if possible
- Indicate date and time collected

### What to Avoid
- Do not contaminate with urine
- Do not collect during menstruation
- Avoid touching feces directly

## Imaging Test Preparation

### X-Ray
- Remove jewelry and metal objects
- Wear loose-fitting clothes
- Inform technician if pregnant
- No fasting required
- No special preparation

### CT Scan
- May require fasting if contrast material needed
- May require special liquid to drink
- Inform of metal implants
- Can wear normal clothes if no metal

### Ultrasound
- May require full bladder (do not urinate)
- Wear loose-fitting clothes
- Some require fasting
- Specific preparation depends on which organ

## Special Situations

### During Menstruation
- Can usually collect urine sample
- Blood draws can proceed (avoid if very heavy)
- Inform staff to note in records

### Medications Affecting Results
- Blood thinners: May need temporary hold
- Steroids: Can affect glucose
- Antibiotics: Can affect liver tests
- Hormones: Can affect thyroid tests
- Caffeine: Can affect heart rate

### Pregnancy or Possible Pregnancy
- Inform provider and lab staff immediately
- Some tests contraindicated in pregnancy
- Inform if breastfeeding (affects medication guidance)

## Result Expectations

### When Results Available
- Blood tests: Usually 24-48 hours
- Urinalysis: Usually 24-48 hours
- Cultures: 48-72 hours or longer
- Imaging: Usually same day or next day

### How to Access Results
- Patient portal (online)
- Phone call from provider
- Scheduled appointment review
- Results mailed if not available electronically

### Understanding Results
- Ask provider to explain results
- Ask what normal range means
- Ask if results require follow-up
- Ask about next steps if abnormal
